A Sacred Mountain

What's On Mount Bromo

The Active Crater

Hike to the rim of the crater and peer into the smoking, sulfuric heart of the volcano. An unforgettable and raw experience of nature's power.

Sea of Sand

A vast expanse of volcanic sand protected since 1919. Crossing this desert-like valley on horseback or by 4x4 jeep is an adventure in itself.

The best spot to watch the sunrise. As the sun rises, it illuminates Mount Semeru (the highest peak in Java) in the distance.

The Legend of Bromo

Geology: Mount Bromo is an active volcano and part of the Tengger massif, in East Java, Indonesia. At 2,329 meters (7,641 ft), it is not the highest peak in the massif, but it is the most well-known.

Etymology: The name "Bromo" comes from the Javanese pronunciation of the Hindu creator god, Brahma.

Mythology: According to folklore, the local Tengger people believe that Mount Bromo is a sacred site. The legend tells of Roro Anteng and Joko Seger, who prayed to the gods for children.

Tradition: This tradition is continued today in the annual Yadnya Kasada festival where offerings are thrown into the crater to protect the people from disaster.
